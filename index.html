1. Problem Statement

1.1 The Coordination Failure

Mexico has over 80 million pets (69.8% of households). The pet services market is valued at $4.5 billion USD in 2025 and projected to reach $6.3 billion by 2032.

Yet the information infrastructure is deeply fragmented. Vaccination records are lost, veterinary histories are siloed, and adoptions lack a verifiable trail. Trust is rebuilt from scratch every time a pet crosses a border or changes vets.

1.2 Why Existing Solutions Fail

Centralized databases (municipal registries, corporate systems) fail because they have a single controller whose incentives do not align with all actors. They can change access rules, charge fees, or get hacked. A DAO-governed protocol on Ethereum eliminates this root problem via public, auditable code.

2. DAO Theory Applied

A Decentralized Autonomous Organization is not just a voting tool; it is a coordination mechanism for actors who share an interest but lack common trust infrastructure. PAW Chain fits this structurally perfectly.

No prior relationship

A pet owner in CDMX and a vet in Monterrey have no shared history. Without a shared protocol, trust is impossible.

Valid distrust exists

Paper can be forged. Private databases altered. On-chain credentials signed with a private key cannot be tampered with.

No central authority

PETCO, airlines, SENASICA, and vets have different incentives. A DAO ensures no single entity dictates terms to the others.

3. Protocol Design

Layer 1 · Digital Identity (ERC-721 Base)

Unique NFT on Ethereum L2. IPFS via Pinata for permanent metadata. Cryptographically tied to the owner's wallet.

Layer 2 · Verifiable Credentials

DAO-authorized vets issue signed credentials (vaccines, surgeries) forming an authentic, tamper-resistant history.

Layer 3 · Travel Clearance

Scan a QR code to view verified on-chain data. Impossible to forge. Eliminates 15-day paper expiration panic.

Layer 4 · DAO Governance

Rules voted via PAW Improvement Proposals (PIPs) using Snapshot off-chain wallet signatures.

Scope Definition

Included in Pilot

  • NFT identity on Base Mainnet
  • IPFS metadata & QR verification
  • Travel Clearance demo (SENASICA fields)
  • On-chain ownership transfer history
  • DAO wallet-based voting (Snapshot)

Out of Scope

  • Integration with existing clinic software
  • Insurance or financial instruments
  • Speculative tokenomics
  • Commercial launch to general public
